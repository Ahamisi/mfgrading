📖 Les Artistes — Scoring Rules
Universal scoring rules (apply to every task)
  • Every task gets exactly one 1 (worst) and exactly one 7 (best). Never tie on 1 or 7.
  • 8-deck task (most common): use 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 with exactly one two-way tie. No duplicates at extremes.
  • 9+ decks: additional two-way ties allowed. No tie may be 3-way or larger.
  • Fewer than 7 decks: no duplicate scores. Use gaps to show quality differences (e.g., {1, 2, 5, 7} shows the top is significantly better).
  • Space scores across the full 1–7 range to show real variability within the task.
  • A/B Preferences and Deck Outlines are pairwise picks, not 1–7 rankings.
  • Do NOT validate factual claims through external research. Do NOT translate tasks.
  • Cite the specific slide number and the specific visual element in every observation.
Major errors to flag (highest to lowest priority)
  1. Text or slide elements overflowing or overlapping
  2. Empty or broken shapes (broken arrows, arrows pointing nowhere)
  3. Awkward alignment and spacing issues
  4. Inconsistency across slides (unexpected background changes, different header fonts)
  5. Illegible text (poor contrast, very small text)
  6. Slides too text-heavy, dense, wordy, or cluttered
  7. Charts or graphs visually broken or malformed
Weigh holistically. A deck with one major high-priority issue may still rank above a deck with many minor issues. Consider severity, frequency, and overall impact.
Voice rules (all rationales)
  • Neutral and objective — no first-person pronouns
  • No em dashes. No word "faithful" (unless referring to source material in Content Grading).
  • No buzzwords: cohesive, seamless, robust, leverages, vibrant, dynamic, sophisticated
  • Every observation must cite a slide number and a specific element
  • Strong: "Slide 4 has consistent spacing between content blocks" — Weak: "The polish is bad"
